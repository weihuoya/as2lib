The Project's Workflow:

  1. Define Technical Purpose

    The team has an idea for a package and plans the creation:
    • Why does the as2lib need this package?
    • Are there connections to other packages?
    • Do we have to split it into different packages?.

  2. Definition of the Package

    The team works out the definition of the package (some kind of raw scribble)
    • Name of the package?
    • Integration of existing stuff?
    • Package rules.
    • Package scope.

  3. Definition of a Package-Master (PM)

    The team defines the head developer of this package.
    • The PM coordinates the discussion on the package within the team.
    • The PM has a complete overview over the package.
    • The PM defines the classes, interfaces and the hierarchy of the package.
    • The PM stays in contact to other PM's to avoid overlapping and guarantee optimal cooperation with other packages.
    • The PM coordinates the developers working on the package.
    • The PM will care and decide about everything concerning the package.

  4. Define Classes and Interfaces of the Package

    The package is filled with functionality by the team managed by the PM.
    • Which classes and interfaces are needed for what pourpose?
    • How do they extend and implement other classes and interfaces (hierarchy)?
    5. Result: Plan of the package.

  5. Design of Classes and Interfaces

    The PM starts a discussion on the design of the classes and interfaces of the package
    • What methods does the class/interface need and what is their pourpose and usage?
    • What properties does the class need and what is their pourpose and usage?
    • What exceptions are thrown?
    Result: Sketch of the classes and interfaces.

  6. Start coding

    Now that all is defined the developers can start coding!
    • Who is coding what?
    • When will it be ready?
